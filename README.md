# 🔍 Swiggy Customer Retention Case Study

## 📌 Overview

This project analyzes a real-world customer retention problem at Swiggy using Business Analysis and Product Thinking frameworks. The objective is to identify why new customers stop ordering after their first few purchases and recommend data-driven solutions that improve long-term customer retention.

---

# 🏢 Company

**Swiggy**  
Industry: Food Delivery & Quick Commerce  
Business Type: eCommerce Marketplace

---

# 📖 Business Problem

Swiggy observed that although daily order volume remained strong, a significant percentage of new customers became inactive after placing only one or two orders.

### Key Challenges

- 40% of new users never place a second order within 30 days.
- 65% of returning users only come back with discounts.
- Delivery-related customer support tickets increased by 18%.
- Teams were working independently without shared success metrics.
- Revenue per user was declining despite healthy order volume.

---

# 🎯 Business Objective

Improve customer retention by identifying the root causes of customer churn and creating a sustainable growth strategy that increases repeat orders while reducing dependency on discounts.

---

# 📊 Dataset / Information Provided

The case study was based on the following business information:

- New user retention rate
- Repeat purchase behavior
- Customer support ticket trends
- Delivery experience metrics
- Discount usage
- Revenue per user
- Daily order volume
- Cross-functional team alignment

---

# 🔍 Root Cause Analysis

The analysis identified multiple underlying causes instead of treating low retention as a single problem.

### Customer Issues

- Poor first delivery experience
- Lack of trust
- High dependency on discounts
- Weak customer engagement

### Operational Issues

- Restaurant quality inconsistency
- Delivery delays
- Limited accountability between teams

### Organizational Issues

- Teams working in silos
- Different departments optimizing different metrics
- No shared definition of success

---

# 💡 Recommendations

- Improve first-order customer experience.
- Reduce dependency on promotional discounts.
- Create shared KPIs across Delivery, Restaurant, and Customer Experience teams.
- Improve restaurant quality monitoring.
- Use personalized recommendations after the first purchase.
- Build loyalty programs focused on long-term engagement instead of discounts.
